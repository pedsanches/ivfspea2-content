2) MANUSCRIPT SUBMISSION
============================================================
- Submission implies the work has not been published before, is not under review elsewhere, and has been approved by all co-authors and relevant institutional authorities.
- If authors reuse figures, tables, or text already published elsewhere, they must obtain the necessary permissions for print and online use and provide evidence at submission.
- Submission is done through the journal's online system via the “Submit manuscript” link.
- Authors should upload editable source files at every submission and revision.
- The journal expects manuscript text in common editable formats such as DOCX or LaTeX.
- Author contribution and competing-interest information must be provided in the submission interface; the interface version is the one used in the final publication record.

============================================================
3) TITLE PAGE
============================================================
The title page should include:
- A concise, informative title.
- Full author names.
- Author affiliations (institution, department when applicable, city, state if applicable, country).
- A clear corresponding-author email.
- ORCID iDs when available.

Additional notes:
- If detailed address information is entered with the affiliation, it may be published.
- For temporarily unaffiliated authors, Springer states it generally records city and country rather than publishing email by default.
- Large language models are not treated as authors. If used for substantive assistance, usage should be documented appropriately in the manuscript. Routine AI-assisted copy editing for readability/style does not require declaration, but human authors remain fully responsible for the final text.

Abstract:
- Target length: 150–250 words.
- Avoid undefined abbreviations and unspecified references.

Keywords:
- Provide 4–6 keywords.

Statements and declarations:
- Relevant declarations must appear under a dedicated heading in the manuscript.
- Incomplete submissions may be returned.

============================================================
4) TEXT
============================================================
Formatting:
- Word is the standard submission format.
- Use a normal plain font.
- Use italics only where appropriate.
- Number pages automatically.
- Avoid field functions.
- Use tab stops/formatting tools instead of manual spacing for indents.
- Build tables with the word processor's table tool, not spreadsheets pasted as layout.
- Use an equation editor or MathType for equations.
- Save as DOCX (preferred) or older DOC if necessary.
- Manuscripts with mathematical content may also be submitted in LaTeX; Springer recommends its own LaTeX template.

Headings:
- Use decimal numbering.
- Do not exceed three heading levels.

Abbreviations:
- Define each abbreviation at first use and use it consistently afterward.

Footnotes:
- Use footnotes only for supplementary information.
- Do not use a footnote that is only a bare citation.
- Do not place full bibliographic details in footnotes.
- Do not include figures or tables inside footnotes.
- Use footnotes rather than endnotes.

Acknowledgments:
- Put acknowledgments for people, grants, funds, etc. in a separate section.
- Write funding-organization names in full.

============================================================
5) SCIENTIFIC STYLE
============================================================
- Use internationally accepted SI units.
- Follow standard mathematical notation.
- Typical convention: italics for variables/constants, upright/roman for operators and standard functions, bold for vectors/tensors/matrices.

============================================================
6) REFERENCES
============================================================
Citation style in text:
- Author–year style.
- Examples on the page show standard narrative and parenthetical forms.

Reference-list rules:
- Include only sources cited in the text and already published or accepted for publication.
- Personal communications and unpublished work should usually stay in the main text rather than the reference list.
- Alphabetize by the surname of the first author.
- Ordering rules differ for 1 author, 2 authors, and 3+ authors; then sort chronologically as appropriate.
- Include DOI links whenever available.
- Standard journal-title abbreviations are preferred; when uncertain, the full journal title is acceptable.

The page also provides examples for:
- Journal articles
- DOI-only articles
- Books
- Book chapters
- Online documents
- Dissertations

============================================================
7) TABLES
============================================================
- Number tables with Arabic numerals.
- Cite tables in the text in numerical order.
- Every table should have a caption explaining the table.
- Previously published material must be credited at the end of the caption.
- Table footnotes should use superscript lower-case letters; asterisks may be used for significance/statistical notes.

============================================================
8) ARTWORK AND ILLUSTRATIONS
============================================================
General electronic figure submission:
- Submit all figures electronically.
- Indicate the software used to create the artwork.
- Preferred formats: EPS for vector graphics, TIFF for halftones; Microsoft Office formats are also accepted.
- Embed fonts in vector files.
- Name files as Fig + number (example pattern: Fig1.xxx).

Resolution guidance:
- Line art: minimum 1200 dpi.
- Halftone art: minimum 300 dpi.
- Combination art: minimum 600 dpi.

Line-art expectations:
- Black-and-white graphics with no shading.
- Avoid faint lines and faint lettering.
- Keep lines sufficiently thick for final reproduction.

Color art / lettering / sizing:
- Color is free online.
- If print will be black and white, ensure the figure remains understandable without color.
- Do not mention colors in captions when the print version will be black and white.
- Submit color artwork in RGB.
- Use simple, legible sans-serif lettering.
- Keep font size consistent.
- Avoid decorative effects.
- Do not place titles/captions inside the figure itself.

Figure placement and captions:
- Number figures consecutively.
- Cite figures in order.
- Use figure parts labels consistently when needed.
- Each figure needs a caption.
- Captions should explain the figure without forcing the reader back into the main text for basic interpretation.

Accessibility:
- Provide descriptive captions.
- Avoid relying only on color to convey information.
- Maintain strong contrast in lettering.

Generative AI images:
- The page points authors to Springer's policy on generative AI images.

============================================================
9) SUPPLEMENTARY INFORMATION (SI)
============================================================
- Supplementary files can include multimedia and other supporting materials for online publication.
- Research data should preferably be placed in repositories when possible; authors are advised to check the journal's research-data policy before using SI for datasets.

Submission expectations:
- Use standard file formats.
- Include identifying information in each file (article title, journal, author names, corresponding-author affiliation/email as applicable).
- Keep downloadability in mind for large files.

Examples of supported material on the page:
- Audio/video/animations
- Text/presentations (PDF preferred)
- Spreadsheets (.csv or .xlsx)
- Specialized formats (examples include .pdb, .wrl, .nb, .tex)
- Bundled archives (.zip or .gz)

Accessibility:
- Supplementary files should also be accessible.
- The page specifically notes descriptive captions and avoidance of rapid flashing in video content.

10) EDITING SERVICES
============================================================
- Springer offers optional author services such as English editing, formatting, figure preparation, translation, and related support.
- Use of these services is optional and does not guarantee acceptance.

11) ETHICAL RESPONSIBILITIES OF AUTHORS
============================================================
The page states the journal follows COPE principles and expects good scientific practice. Main points include:
- Do not submit the same manuscript to multiple journals at once.
- Do not present data or results in a misleading way.
- Retain data/documentation so results can be verified if requested.
- Be prepared to supply supporting data or records if questions arise, except where confidentiality/proprietary restrictions apply.
- If misconduct or fraud is suspected, the journal/publisher may investigate using COPE-based procedures.
- Authors should correct significant errors discovered after publication.
- The page also mentions reviewer suggestions/exclusions and broader publication-integrity expectations.

============================================================
12) AUTHORSHIP PRINCIPLES
============================================================
- Authors should have made a meaningful contribution to the work.
- The corresponding author acts on behalf of all co-authors and is responsible for integrity/communication obligations.
- All listed authors should approve the manuscript before submission.
- Author order and author-group composition should be correct at submission.
- Adding or removing authors during revision is generally discouraged and may require detailed justification and editorial approval.
- ORCID use is recommended.
- Springer recommends contribution statements where appropriate.

============================================================
13) COMPLIANCE WITH ETHICAL STANDARDS / FUNDING / DECLARATIONS
============================================================
- The corresponding author should ensure that required disclosures and declarations are included.
- Funding statements should be explicit.
- The page provides example wording both for funded work and for cases with no funding.
- Blanket statements may be used when all authors share the same conflict/funding status.

============================================================
14) COMPETING INTERESTS
============================================================
- Authors must disclose financial and non-financial interests related directly or indirectly to the submitted work.
- Interests from the previous 3 years should normally be reported, and older interests may still need disclosure if readers could reasonably view them as relevant.
- The page includes sample wording for both positive disclosures and “nothing to declare” cases.
- The Editor-in-Chief may reject submissions that do not follow these disclosure expectations.

============================================================
15) AFTER ACCEPTANCE
============================================================
- Accepted articles move to production/typesetting.
- Authors receive follow-up emails concerning affiliation confirmation, publication model, rights/payment issues where applicable, and proofs.
- Proof corrections are intended for typesetting/conversion/completeness issues.
- Major content changes, title changes, or authorship changes are not normally allowed at proof stage without editorial approval.
- After online publication, later changes are usually handled through an erratum/correction process.
- The article may appear Online First and becomes citable via DOI before print issue assignment.

============================================================
16) OPEN CHOICE / OPEN ACCESS
============================================================
- The journal offers Springer Open Choice for open-access publication.
- APCs vary by journal.
- The page highlights visibility, accessibility, and mandate-compliance advantages.
- Under Open Choice, copyright usually remains with the author(s), with publication under a Creative Commons licence according to Springer terms.

============================================================
17) RESEARCH DATA POLICY / DATA AVAILABILITY
============================================================
- The journal follows Springer's research-data policy.
- Original research articles must include a Data Availability Statement.
- The statement should explain how supporting data can be accessed, including links/citations to archived datasets when relevant.
- If public sharing is not possible, the statement should explain access conditions and reuse limits.
- Discipline-specific repositories are recommended where available; some data types may require mandatory repository deposition.
- Publicly available datasets supporting the work should be cited formally, ideally with persistent identifiers such as DOIs.
- If a journal uses double-anonymous review, authors should also consider anonymizing authorship cues in shared data resources.
- Springer provides a research-data helpdesk and FAQ for support.

19) PRACTICAL CHECKLIST FOR YOUR PAPER
============================================================
Before submission, verify at least the following:
- Title page complete
- Abstract between 150 and 250 words
- 4 to 6 keywords
- Author affiliations and corresponding email correct
- ORCIDs added where available
- All declarations included
- References in author–year format and alphabetized
- DOI links added where possible
- Tables and figures cited in order
- Figure resolutions/formats acceptable
- Permissions collected for reused material
- Data Availability Statement included
- Funding and competing-interest statements included
- Editable source files ready for upload
